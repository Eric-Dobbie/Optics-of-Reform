City Police Launch "Project Response" to Address Officer Shortage
By Staff Reports | METRO CITY
The Metropolitan Police Department announced today that it is launching "Project Response," a mass hiring initiative aimed at expanding the number of officers on the force.
Citing research that fully staffed departments build stronger community safety, the department plans to fill over 50 sworn officer positions this fiscal year. Officials stated the goal is to modernize the force and ensure the department has the capacity to serve the city it protects.
"Modern policing requires adequate staffing. Research shows that departments with full officer capacity are more effective at reducing response times and improving safety outcomes for everyone. We need qualified candidates ready to serve and protect."

— Chief Michael Smith, Metropolitan Police
